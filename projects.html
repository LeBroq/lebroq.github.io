Ardenfell 
Team Size: 6                Tools:

Status: Complete            Unreal Engine 5, GitHub, Blender, FMOD

Ardenfell is a 3rd person souls-like game which was created as my 
independent study semester-long project. The game aims to mimic 
certain elements of a typical Dark Souls games, such as the leveling
systems, play style, and summoning systems for multiplayer.

I took on the role of managing the player camera, and its lock on 
functionality, as well as the summoning system, including the 
networking and replication that it would entail. In addition, due
to having the mostexperience with Dark Souls games, I also acted 
as a resource for my teammates to double check their work and 
although my team was unable to complete a full level, I believe 
we had come out with a decent base to expand upon in the future. 
I was able to learn a lot about Unreal Engine's networking and 
Gameplay Ability System as well as producing a game with a 
multi-talented team.
                        
Responsibilities:

  • Game Designer
  • Multiplayer System Designer/Programming
  • Camera Programming
  • Player Programming Support
The InnBetween 
Team Size: 3                Tools:
Status: Complete            Unreal Editor for Fortnite (UEFN)

The InnBetween is a Fortnite island created during  one week with
a small team as a part of the Triangle Game Jam. The game focuses
on maintaining a restaurant by trying to keep up with customer 
orders, preparemeals, and deliver the meals to the customers with
a limited amount of hands.

I took on the role of restricting the player and ensuring they
didn't have access to particular elements normally found in 
Fortnite, such as limiting the amount of items, in this case,
ingredients, so they would have to make multiple trips and plan 
their cooking route. In addition, I also helped track the food 
the player was holding so they could perform their food deliveries. 
The project was a great opportunity to further my skill and 
knowledge of Verse, the programming language used within UEFN, as 
well as get to know more of the creative devices that UEFN has to 
offer.
                        
Responsibilities:

  • Game Designer
  • Level Designer
  • Player Programming
Educational Game Creation Tool 
Team Size: 5                Tools:
Status: Complete            Godot 4, GitHub

The Educational Game Creation Tool acted as my Senior Design final
project. The College of Business at North Carolina State University
acted as our sponsor and asked for a tool that they could use that
could create small education visual novel type games that could be
used to grade assignments. My team decided to create a plugin 
within Godot that would take away most to all of the complexities
of the engine and make it solely be able to make these types of 
games with ease of use for teachers and non-game developers.

I took on the role of managing images that could be added to our 
system. I allowed for a user to insert any image they would want 
and use them as either background images or graphs for a particular 
question or scene. In addition, I worked on connecting our tool to 
our exporting tool so the games in the tool would correctly reflect 
on the output game. The project acted as a great opportunity to act 
as a company working with clients to create and iterate upon a 
program. We used an agile approach and met with the client weekly 
to see what they liked and what needed changes or improvements.
                        
Responsibilities:

  • Tool Programmer
  • System Programmer

Galaxian 3D 
Team Size: 4                Tools:
Status: Complete            Unreal Engine 5, GitHub

Galaxian 3D is a 3D variant of the Galaxian arcade game made as 
my Computer Graphics final project. My team tried to retain as 
much of the original gameplay mechanics and systems as much as 
we could, while also attemtping to add a bit of challenge due to
the 3D space. A few changes we made were to have the enemies 
surround the player as opposed to be in front of them to make it 
more of a dog fight and we made one type of enemy progressively 
get harder to deal with that attempted to learn as levels 
progressed. 

My role in the project was to implement all but the learning 
enemies behavior. I learned and utilized Unreal Engine 5's 
behavior trees to create different variants of ships, such as 
escorts, fighters, and the galboss and had them mimic their 
original Galaxian behavior.
                        
Responsibilities:

  • Enemy Programming
The Lost Shadow 
Team Size: 5                Tools:
Status: Complete            Unreal Engine 5, GitHub, Blender

The Lost Shadow is a 3D puzzle game made for a Video Game 
Development Club at NCState's semester game jam. The goal of the
game is to utilize the ability to move objects around a light source 
and swap between controlling the character and it's shadow to finish
a level. Objects can be moved around a 3D space and be utilized to 
create platforms with the shadows they cast for the players shadow 
to use.

Due to the heavy classwork at the time, for this project I had more 
of a design and tester role. I mainly helped to ideate many of the 
game components and helped to debug any issues my teammates had in 
their code.
                        
Responsibilities:

  • Game Design
  • Tester
1v1 Arena King of the Hill

Kaching 
Team Size: 4                Tools:
Status: Completed           Godot 4

Kaching is a 2D top-down zombie survival game. Kaching was my first 
major game project upon entering college and my first time 
participating in a game jam. The main mechanic of the game is that 
coins are your ammo. In order to get more coins zombies must be 
defeated to drop dollar bills, which will then allow you to purchase 
upgrades at vending machines. Vending machines will then dispense 
your change which acts as your ammo resupply.

Due to this being my first game jam, and first time using a game 
engine for a project, I kept my role small and mainly focused on 
coming up with and iterating on our teams game idea, as well as 
implementing small aspects of the player character.
                        
Responsibilities:

  • Game Design
  • Programmer
